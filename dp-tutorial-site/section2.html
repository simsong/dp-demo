Noise and Accuracy

In the last example, we saw how noise injection protected the privacy of a family. With noise, we have obscured the familyâ€™s data, but now the results are slightly less accurate.



To visualize how privacy and accuracy are related, and how noise affects data, play with our interactive simulator.



Because of the noise, some statistics will be reported with values larger than they would been if they had been computed using the confidential data, while other statistics will be reported with smaller values. These differences are potentially quite large for small geographic areas, like a single block, in order to assure data confidentiality.

But remember, the Census Bureau collects data on the entire country, counting over 300 million people in over 11 million census blocks.

At larger geographic levels like census tracts, counties and states, often with millions of residents, the differences between the statistics computed with the confidential data and the public data are insignificant. As a result, the data publications of the 2020 decennial census data will be accurate for the needs of data users.
