Preventing Database Reconstruction

The Census Bureau has adopted differential privacy and noise injection to protect against the privacy risk of database reconstruction.. This risk exists when exact statistics based on confidential data are published: the more statistics that are published, the greater the risk.

To understand the risk of database reconstruction, consider the reported data for a fictional apartment building, without noise added:

Reported Data With Noise
Male Count:3
Female Count:5
Avg. Age:25

With just these statistics, it is hard to guess which people might live here. It could be 7 people who are all 25 years old, or perhaps several seniors and several young adults.

But what happens when we add more statistics to the published data?

Reported Statistics
Based on Confidential Data
Male Count:3
Female Count:5
Avg. Age:25
Male Median Age:30
Male Avg. Age:44

With additional queries released in the published data, it is possible to learn more about the raw data:

  • In this case, there are only 30 different combinations of three ages for which the median is 30 and the average is 44.
  • The table also reveals that one of the males is age 30, because that is the median age.
  • If there is another dataset (or even a newspaper article) about this apartment building which states that the oldest resident is 80 and male, then we know that the ages of the males are 22, 30 and 80.

Reported Statistics
With on Noise Injection
Male Count:3
Female Count:5
Avg. Age:30
Male Median Age:31
Male Avg. Age:42

It turns out that there are 31 combinations of ages for which the median is 31 and the average is 42. But this doesn't tell us anything about the actual people living in the apartment building, because of the injected noise. It might be the case that one of the males is aged 31. Then again, the true number might have been 30, or 29, or even 38. A person trying to discover the underlying confidential data has know way of knowing.

Without knowledge of how much noise has been introduced to a residence, it is not possible to make accurate guesses about the raw data. Thus, injecting noise protects the individuals in the residence.
