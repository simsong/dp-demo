Data Privacy in 2020

As we’ve learned, adding noise to a set of data involves small, random changes to published statistics. These changes protect every single American’s data by obscuring individual data in a geographic area. The noise balances more at higher geographic levels, and creates an accurate data set for the 2020 Census data releases.

In the 2020 Census, protecting privacy is more important than ever before. As the nation’s largest federal statistical agency, the U.S. Census Bureau takes our responsibility to ensure the safety of sensitive data extremely seriously. We also pride ourselves on openness and transparency, and hope this guide has helped educate about how we care for your census data.
